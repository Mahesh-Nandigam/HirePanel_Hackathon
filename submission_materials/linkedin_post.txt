Are your AI agents stuck in Groundhog Day?
Every time you give them a new task, they completely forget what they learned from the last one.

I got tired of stateless agents making the exact same mistakes over and over, so I rebuilt our AI hiring committee to actually remember things. 

Here is what I learned building a stateful multi-agent system:

1. Memory beats prompt engineering. 
Telling an agent to "be strict" in a system prompt works for one turn. But when you embed Hindsight memory, the agent actually recalls past candidate evaluations. It calibrates its own strictness based on historical data. Authentic behavior change > prompt hacking.

2. You don't need 70B for everything. 
Running a full debate between a Tech Lead agent and an HR agent on Llama-3.3-70B is expensive. I used cascadeflow to intercept the LLM calls at runtime. It routes simple resume parsing to the cheap 8B model and saves the 70B heavy-lifting strictly for the debate. Token costs dropped by 75%.

3. Stateful agents are the future.
Once you see an agent reference a past interaction to make a better decision today, stateless agents feel completely broken.
